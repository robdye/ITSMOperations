# Agent Ownership Selection Criteria

## Who Should Own an Agent?

Agent ownership is a governance responsibility, not just a technical role.
The owner is accountable for:

- Ongoing maintenance and updates
- Compliance with Agent Governance Policy AGP-001
- Responding to incidents or security concerns
- Renewal of approval in the Approved Agent Registry
- Knowledge of the agent's purpose, capabilities, and data access

## Selection Priority

When selecting a new owner, follow this priority order:

### Priority 1: Subject Matter Expert
- Someone who understands the business process the agent supports
- Ideally from the same team/department as the departing employee
- Must have M365 Copilot license

### Priority 2: Technical Owner
- A developer or admin who can maintain the agent's configuration
- Understands the agent's plugins, actions, and data sources
- Can respond to technical incidents

### Priority 3: Manager (Interim)
- The departing employee's direct manager
- Serves as interim owner while a permanent replacement is identified
- Maximum interim period: 30 days

### Priority 4: IT Governance (Last Resort)
- IT Governance team takes ownership
- Creates a ticket for permanent reassignment
- May recommend decommission if no suitable owner found within 60 days

## Ownership Requirements

The new owner MUST:
1. ☐ Have an active M365 Copilot license
2. ☐ Complete agent governance training (or commit to within 30 days)
3. ☐ Acknowledge ownership responsibilities via email
4. ☐ Review the agent's current configuration and compliance status
5. ☐ Update the Approved Agent Registry with their details

## Ownership Anti-Patterns

Avoid these common mistakes:
- **Shared mailbox ownership**: Agents must have an individual owner, not a team
- **Manager chain ownership**: Don't assign to someone 3+ levels removed
- **Cross-department without context**: Don't assign to someone who doesn't understand the agent's purpose
- **Temporary contractors**: Avoid assigning to people on fixed-term contracts
